    Enter the battery number

    The battery number is printed on a label on the battery itself.

    Where to find it: Look for a code on the top or side label of your battery. Common formats include DIN66MF, NS70, N70ZZ, LN2, or a manufacturer part number.
    Enter exactly as it appears on the label
    Please enter the battery number, or go back and choose a different option.

    Common Australian formats include:

    • DIN/BCI numbers — e.g. DIN66MF, N70ZZ, NS40ZL
    • Group numbers — e.g. 24, 27, 31, 34/78
    • LN codes — e.g. LN0, LN2, LN3, LN4 (used in modern European vehicles)
    • Manufacturer codes — vary by brand; enter exactly as printed

    Supporting battery details

    These details improve the quality of your preliminary result. Leave blank if unknown.

    Most automotive/marine batteries are 12 V. Deep-cycle may be 6 V or 12 V. EV packs may be 24 V, 36 V or 48 V.
    CCA is a measure of how much current a battery can deliver for 30 seconds at −18 °C without dropping below 7.2 V. A higher number means better cold-start performance. You can find the CCA rating on the top label of most car batteries.
    Battery chemistry / type
    • Flooded lead-acid (FLA) — traditional, low cost, requires maintenance
    • AGM (Absorbent Glass Mat) — sealed, spill-proof, suits stop-start vehicles
    • Gel — deep-cycle, good vibration resistance
    • Lithium (LiFePO4) — lightweight, long life, higher cost
    • Not sure — select this if you are unsure
    Length × Width × Height in mm — e.g. 275 × 175 × 175

    Upload battery photos

    Photos help us identify the battery when a number is not available.

    What to photograph — include all of these if possible:

    • Full battery label — the complete sticker on top or side showing all printed text
    • Terminal position — show which terminal is positive (+) and which is negative (−), and their locations on the battery
    • Model or part numbers — any codes, batch numbers, or manufacturer identifiers printed on the case or label
    • Equipment the battery came from — photograph the battery still in (or next to) the equipment so we can see the mounting bay and connections
    ⚠ Prototype limitation: File uploads are not processed in this prototype. Attach photos to your enquiry email instead — we will ask for these in the confirmation step.
